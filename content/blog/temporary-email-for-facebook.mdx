## Why Use a Temporary Email for Facebook?

### 1. Privacy Protection
Facebook tracks everything — your likes, comments, friend lists, and browsing habits. When you sign up with your personal email, Facebook can:
- Link your email to your real identity across platforms
- Send your data to advertisers
- Use your email for "People You May Know" suggestions
- Expose your email in data breaches (Facebook had a breach affecting 533 million users)

### 2. Avoid Facebook Marketing Spam
Once Facebook has your email, expect:
- "You have new notifications" emails
- Friend suggestion emails
- Event invitation emails
- Marketplace alerts
- "People are looking for you" emails

With a temporary email, all this goes to a disposable inbox — not your personal one.

### 3. Create Alt or Anonymous Accounts
There are legitimate reasons to have separate Facebook accounts:
- **Business page management** without personal exposure
- **Privacy from specific people** (stalkers, harassers)
- **Testing** Facebook features or ads
- **Marketplace selling** without sharing personal details

### 4. Recover from a Compromised Account
If your Facebook account was hacked and the email changed, creating a new account with a temp email lets you start fresh without exposing your real email again.
