## The Privacy Problem with Gmail

Gmail is a Google product, and Google's business model is fundamentally **advertising based on your data**. Here's what most users don't realize:

### What Google Collects Through Gmail

- **Email content scanning** — Google scans email content for ad targeting and "smart" features
- **Cross-service tracking** — Your Gmail activity is correlated with YouTube, Search, Maps, and Android usage
- **Third-party data sharing** — Apps connected via "Sign in with Google" get broad data access
- **Metadata collection** — Who you email, when, how often, and from where
- **Purchase tracking** — Google automatically track purchases from confirmation emails
- **Travel tracking** — Flight and hotel bookings are extracted and added to Google services

### The Data Breach Factor

With 1.8 billion Gmail users, Gmail addresses are the most commonly found in data breach databases:
- Your Gmail appears in multiple breaches simply because it's used everywhere
- Credential stuffing attacks target Gmail addresses first
- Phishing campaigns overwhelmingly target Gmail users
- Once your Gmail is compromised, attackers access your entire Google ecosystem

With disposable email, none of this applies. There's no account, no tracking, no data retention, and no identity to compromise.
