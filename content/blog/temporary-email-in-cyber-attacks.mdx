# Temporary Email and Cyber Attacks: Your First Line of Defense

In 2025 alone, **over 8 billion records were exposed** through data breaches. Email addresses are the #1 target in these breaches — because your email is the key to your entire digital life.

Temporary email addresses provide a critical layer of defense against cyber attacks. Here's how.

## How Your Email Gets You Hacked

### The Attack Chain
Most cyber attacks follow the same pattern:

1. **Data breach** → Your email is leaked
2. **Credential stuffing** → Hackers try your email + common passwords on other sites
3. **Phishing emails** → Targeted emails to your leaked address
4. **Account takeover** → Hackers gain access to your accounts
5. **Identity theft** → Your personal data is exploited

**The starting point is always your email address.** Remove that, and the entire chain breaks.

| **Phishing** | 3.4 billion attempts/day | Delivered via email |

| **Business email compromise** | $2.7 billion in losses | Exploits email trust |
| **Ransomware** | 236 million attacks | Often starts with email |

## How Temporary Email Protects You

### 1. Breaks the Data Breach Chain

When you sign up for a website with a temporary email:
- If the site gets breached, **your real email isn't in the data**
- Hackers can't use the leaked temp email for credential stuffing
- No connection between the breached data and your real identity

### 2. Makes Phishing Impossible

Phishing emails only work when sent to **an email you actually check.** With temporary email:
- Phishing emails go to a disposable inbox you'll never see again
- No way for attackers to reach your real inbox
- Even spear phishing becomes impossible without your real email

### 3. Prevents Credential Stuffing

Credential stuffing relies on leaked email+password combinations. If you use a temporary email:
- The leaked email is disposable and already forgotten
- No password reuse risk since the temp email has no password
- Your real email is never in any breach database

### 4. Stops Social Engineering

Social engineers use leaked email addresses to:
- Impersonate you
- Contact your friends/colleagues
- Build profiles using your email across services

Temporary email breaks this by providing no real data to exploit.

### 5. Blocks Email Tracking

Marketers and hackers use tracking pixels in emails to:
- Confirm your email is active
- Track when and where you open emails
- Determine your location and device

With a temporary inbox, tracking data is meaningless — it can't be tied back to you.
